Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 18, 2026

tunn ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our service.

Information We Collect

When you use tunn, we collect:

  • GitHub Account Information: When you log in with GitHub, we receive your email address(es) and basic profile information.
  • Tunnel Usage Data: We track bandwidth usage per account to enforce rate limits.
  • Server Logs: We collect standard server logs including IP addresses, request times, and HTTP headers for security and debugging purposes.

How We Use Your Information

  • Authentication: Your email addresses are used to verify your identity and control access to tunnels.
  • Rate Limiting: Usage data ensures fair use of the service.
  • Security: Logs help us detect and prevent abuse.
  • Communication: We may send service-related emails (account issues, security alerts).

Information We Don't Collect

  • We do not inspect, store, or log the content of your tunnel traffic.
  • We do not sell or share your personal information with third parties for marketing.
  • We do not use tracking cookies or analytics services.

Data Retention

We retain your account information as long as your account is active. Usage logs are retained for 30 days. You can request deletion of your data by contacting us.

Data Security

All tunnel traffic is encrypted using TLS. We use industry-standard security measures to protect your data.

Open Source

tunn is open source software. You can review our code on GitHub to verify our privacy practices. You can also self-host tunn if you prefer complete control over your data.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date.

Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at privacy@tunn.to.
